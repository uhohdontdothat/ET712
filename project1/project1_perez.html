LION

Adults, Please read this to the child!

X

A lion is a big, strong cat that lives in Africa and some parts of India. Boy lions have fluffy manes around their heads, but girl lions don’t. They live in families called prides and work together to hunt for food. Lions roar really loud—you can hear them from miles away! They love to nap and can sleep for up to 20 hours a day. Even though they are called the "king of the jungle," lions actually live in open grasslands, not jungles!

PEACOCK

Adults, Please read this to the child!

X

A peacock is a beautiful bird with big, colorful feathers. Only boy peacocks have the long, shiny tail, and they spread it out to show off! Girl peacocks are called peahens, and they have shorter, brownish feathers. Peacocks make loud calls that sound like "meow!" even though they aren’t cats. They can fly, but only for short distances, like up into trees. Their feathers don’t help them fly, but they sure make them look fancy!

PENGUIN

Adults, Please read this to the child!

X

A penguin is a bird, but it can’t fly in the sky. Instead, it swims really fast in the water using its wings like flippers! Some penguins live in super cold places like Antarctica, but other penguins live in warm places. Penguins love to slide on their bellies on the ice—it’s like they’re playing! Baby penguins, called chicks, have soft, fuzzy feathers to keep them warm. Some penguin dads even help keep the eggs warm by balancing them on their feet!

SHARK

Adults, Please read this to the child!

X

A shark is a big fish that lives in the ocean. It has lots of sharp teeth to help it catch its food. Sharks have strong bodies, but they don’t have bones like people do—they have something soft inside that helps them swim fast. Some sharks, like the great white, are very big, while others, like the dogfish, are smaller. Sharks never really sleep; they rest one part of their brain while still swimming. Even though sharks look scary, most of them are not dangerous to people!
